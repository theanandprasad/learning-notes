Sandboxes Are the Servers of the Harness Era

By Aparna Dhinakaran — Co-founder & CPO, Arize AI • Published Apr 19, 2026 • Saved May 31, 2026
AI Agents Sandboxes / Infra State & Trajectories Market Structure

The harness is the application. The sandbox is the server.

The central analogy

An application runs on a server — it uses the compute, writes to the filesystem, maintains state across restarts. The harness ↔ sandbox relationship is the same: the harness reasons and calls tools; the sandbox provides the isolated execution environment. Swap either one independently and the system still works.

Zooms out from the harness internals (What is an Agent Harness) to the infrastructure layer beneath it, and sets up the swarm thesis developed in Swarm Management.

Where it started: evals

Sandboxes began as disposable test environments. An entire cottage industry of eval companies sells benchmarks to labs like Anthropic and OpenAI — and step one in proving an eval works is proving a coding agent struggles against it. Isolation matters: coding agents can use the internet to cheat, so the sandbox provides the air gap.

Where it's heading: long-running harnesses

The more interesting use case is harnesses that run for hours — fixing software, writing code across a large codebase, running analytical reports, operating parts of your business continuously. Most tasks worth doing are not one-and-done.

Running a harness continuously is hard. Anthropic's post on managed agents names the core problems: sandboxes die mid-task, state must survive failures, the environment must be robust enough for hours of work. The harness stops being disposable and starts being a job you depend on.

The state question: what makes a harness restartable?

Two things:

Who controls the state?

If trajectory data is the valuable asset, sandbox providers aren't just selling compute — they're selling the environment in which this data is generated. Two market futures:

Labs run managed harnesses end-to-end

Your trajectories live in their session logs. The labs control the state.

Enterprises keep state in-house

Managed harnesses touching production infra is a non-starter. Sandboxes run inside the customer's cloud — via Daytona, E2B, or home-built options like Stripe Minions. State stays in the customer's infrastructure.

Regardless of where the sandbox runs: who owns the trajectory? The reasoning history is what makes a harness's work reproducible, auditable, and improvable. Whoever controls that data has huge leverage in the future AI market.

The future is swarms

The future of the sandbox market is swarms of long-running harnesses — coordinated teams working across many sandboxes, each with its own trajectory and state. Individual harnesses fail and restart; sandboxes die and get reprovisioned; the swarm keeps working. (Seen already in Stripe's Minions and Browserbase's long-running agents.)

The battle lines

The sandbox is the foundation — but the foundation is infra built on sandboxes. Control of the swarm and control of the trajectory data are the two battle lines. Whoever owns the swarm-management layer and the trajectory data will define this market.
