Proof of Abundance… And How to Survive It

By Peter H. Diamandis (@PeterDiamandis) • Published Apr 11, 2026 • Saved Jun 8, 2026
Abundance Exponential Tech Energy Optimism

We forget the world is getting better on so many levels. Abundance isn't coming — it's already in the data, right now.

ℹ️ Context: This piece is adapted from a post promoting the author's new book We Are As Gods: A Survival Guide for the Age of Abundance (with Steven Kotler) and an event. The five statistics below are the figures Diamandis cites; captured here for the argument, not independently verified.

The thesis

Across materials, manufacturing, employment, and computation, the same trajectory keeps repeating: exponential improvement → collapsing costs → expanding access. That's the signature of abundance. Technology creates it; the open question is whether our institutions can distribute it wisely.

Diamandis co-wrote Abundance: The Future Is Better Than You Think (2012) and is following it with We Are As Gods — taking its name from Stewart Brand's 1968 line: "We are as gods — and we might as well get good at it." On his Moonshots podcast he's started collecting recent breakthroughs as ongoing "proof of abundance." Five caught his attention this week — "not because they're flashy, because they're undeniable."

On AI and jobs

The AI-jobs figure is the one Diamandis leans on hardest: 640,000 new US jobs (2023–2025), mostly in categories that didn't exist three years ago — creating work, not just replacing it. He frames it as the recurring pattern of every major transition:

And the flywheel he loves most: robots + AI + energy reinforcing each other. Four Maximo robots autonomously installing solar in the California desert means "Abundance stops being theoretical. It becomes mechanical." It's global, too — Pakistan now generates most of its energy from solar; solar is exploding across Africa.

The pattern — and the real question

Technology vs. institutions

Technology creates abundance

The curves are real and accelerating; the supply problem keeps getting solved by innovation + competition.

Institutions decide who captures it

Markets, governments, legal frameworks determine whether abundance pools at the top or spreads broadly.

"We have the technology. Do we have the wisdom to build institutions that match it?"

The choice

Abundance isn't coming. It's in the data, right now… The only question is whether you see it — or whether you're still watching the Crisis News Network.

Diamandis's close is a posture, not just a forecast: choose to see it, choose to build for it. The future, in his telling, is arriving ahead of schedule — and if you're paying attention, you can see it everywhere.
