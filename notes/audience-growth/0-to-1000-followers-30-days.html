The premise

X is "the only social platform whose code is open source." Every account has a hidden reputation score (TweepCred) derived from PageRank over the social graph. Understand what the code rewards — quality followers, relationship depth, the right formats, and a safe follow ratio — and you can grow on purpose. The author started cold (after building a Shopify app to $800K ARR in silence) and hit 1,000+ followers in ~30 days.

Part 1 — TweepCred: the hidden score that controls reach

Every account has a 0–100 reputation score you never see, derived from PageRank (the same idea Google uses) run across X's entire social graph. Straight from Reputation.scala:

TweepCred = max(0, min(100, round(130 + 5.21 × ln(PageRank))))

Part 2 — The number that kills accounts: 2,500

Follow more than 2,500 accounts and the code applies a TweepCred penalty — up to a 50× reduction (98% of your reputation gone). It's a hard cutoff: 2,499 is safe, 2,501 trips it.

Followers / FollowingReputation penalty
5,000 / 2,400No penalty
5,000 / 3,50030% reduction
3,000 / 5,00092% reduction
1,200 / 15,00098% reduction (effectively zero)

He keeps his following at 491 (a 2.21:1 ratio). If you currently follow more than 2,500, fixing that is the single highest-impact thing you can do today.

Part 3 — Why replies matter more than your tweets

X tracks every relationship via RealGraph: each like, reply, and retweet builds connection strength, which becomes a scoring multiplier.

The daily playbook

What he did

1 post at 8–10 PM IST (US awake) · 70–80 replies (big accounts for reach + builders for relationships) · 10–15 targeted follows/day from #buildinpublic only · zero external links · zero follow/unfollow. Posts with specific numbers ("DR 0→45 in 30 days", "$25K→$50K MRR"), honest struggle posts, and connect posts worked.

What didn't work

Posting 5×/day (the algorithm caps you at 1–2 tweets per timeline load) · generic advice · hashtags · posting when your audience is asleep.

The bottom line: six things the algorithm rewards

1 · Quality followers over quantity — PageRank, not raw follower count
2 · Engagement velocity — the first 15 minutes matter most
3 · Relationship depth — replies that get replies back
4 · Stay under 2,500 following — avoids the catastrophic penalty
5 · Threads and images over links — the format multipliers are real
6 · Consistency in 1–2 topics — topic authority builds over months
