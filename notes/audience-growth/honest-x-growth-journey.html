My Honest X Growth Journey

By baba yaga (@babayagatwt) • Published Jan 29, 2026 • Saved Jun 9, 2026
X / Twitter Growth Build in Public Replies Consistency

A dev's honest, unglamorous growth story: months of posting into the void, a break, then one rule — "even if I get zero engagement, I'll still show up" — that finally turned the corner.

⚠️ Captured as-is. A personal journey; figures (views, follower/impression counts) are the author's own. One developer's honest account, not a guaranteed formula.

The turning point

After two months of active posting with no engagement, growth, or conversations, she set a single rule on restarting: show up even at zero engagement. That's when posts jumped from 300–400 views to 3k–4k. The honest takeaway: "The X algorithm is wild — any random tweet can turn into a banger and hit millions. That's why you keep posting: you never know which one will take off."

The journey, honestly

First: why are you on X?

Decide your reason before anything else — just to grow your network and share your journey, or to earn from it too ("honestly… why not"). Then make your profile intentional, not fancy.

Posting strategy that worked

  • 3–5 posts/day, all inside your niche. Early on, inspired/copied content is okay if it's relevant and engaging.
  • Consistency wins more than volume — and be consistent with timing. Her observed active windows: 11–2 and 4:30–7.
  • Repost your own tweets later; use a pinned tweet + strong bio to hook profile visitors.
  • Verification isn't required but can help credibility later.
  • Engage with similar creators — priority order Comments > DMs > mutual support; thoughtful replies matter more than posting nonstop.
  • Post in niche communities to increase visibility.

How she reached 500 verified followers

Comment under small verified accounts. They notice replies and often follow back if you add value.
Keep comments real. No "nice post," no emoji-only — 1–2 thoughtful lines work best.
Never ask for follow-backs publicly. Low-quality followers hurt your account.
Post quality content alongside commenting. Even low-reach posts act as social proof.
Let bio + pinned tweet close the deal — who you are, what you post, why someone should follow. No extra noise.

The hard part: pushing toward 5M impressions

She was at 2.6M when writing — "purely based on what's working for me":

Stay strict with your niche. Comment only under people in your niche; at most 2 comments per post. Out-of-niche comments might get views but hurt long-term growth.
The reply-guy method. Follow top accounts in your niche, turn on notifications, and when they post leave at most 2 relevant comments — text, occasionally text + GIF/image for attention. "You never know which comment will get traction."
Value comments > random viral comments. Value comments get noticed → get replies → build your name. Random comments may go viral but bring low engagement.
Don't overdo it. No 8–10 posts/day; ≤2 comments per post; avoid out-of-niche. Overdoing floods followers' feeds → mutes/unfollows → "then even with 1000 followers your posts will struggle to cross 20–30 views."
Think long-term. Impressions help monetization; engagement builds network. Balance both.

"Looks good, works bad"

A warning on chasing raw reach: she had a comment with 200k+ views and only ~1% engagement. Low-value comments can ruin your engagement rate even when the view count looks impressive.

Shortcuts work. But they always charge interest later.

Her closing notes — plus a pointed one: "the algorithm doesn't see gender. People do. And that's on them."
