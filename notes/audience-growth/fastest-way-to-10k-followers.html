The core shift

In 2026 X works like TikTok: each post is judged independently (by a Grok-based transformer) and shown to the strangers who should like it — not just your followers. So growth comes from out-of-network reach, which you earn by giving the algorithm niche clarity, high-signal content (reposts & dwell time), and lots of value-replies. Then you do different things at each follower tier.

The topic-cluster rule

ML classifies your account. Post AI one day, cooking the next, politics the third, and the system gets confused. Pick 2–3 tight themes and stay consistent so the algorithm knows which strangers to show you to.

Formats the algorithm pushes now

The rest of the algo formula

  • Be social — reply. You can't grow by only posting; you "interact to be discovered." Write high-value replies on big niche accounts (not "Great post!"). A value reply often gets more impressions than a standalone tweet — especially if the author engages back — funneling people to your profile.
  • Don't put external links. X wants to keep users on-platform; a raw link to your site/newsletter often de-ranks the post. Put the value in the post; use the link only as an optional next step in the replies or at the end of a long-form post.
  • Get verified. Premium gives reach, reply visibility, and search a real push (and unlocks long-form + video weight).

The roadmap: what to do at each follower tier

This is the heart of the playbook — the actions change as you grow:

0–500 — Be a reply guy. Leave 30–100 high-quality "banger" replies daily under accounts you like. X no longer counts the number of replies but scores each one's virality separately, so more good replies = more chances to get caught by the algorithm (and at 0 followers no one reads your posts anyway). Engage more than you post (1–3 posts/week, just to give people a reason to follow). Slide into DMs — ask for advice, a coffee, or just say hi. "People are nicer than you think."
500–5K — Do giveaways. Never give away money (it attracts the worst people). Give away one of three things: a product (Figma/Framer file, GitHub repo, closed-beta access, Notion template), a service (extended trials, free Pro subs), or knowledge (a useful PDF guide — this very article was a 10K giveaway). Giveaways pull attention and can funnel to your business.
5K–10K — Reverse-engineer winners. Use X Advanced Search (or the @superx_so extension) to browse the biggest accounts in your niche and study their most popular posts (500–1K+ likes). Take inspiration, don't steal — e.g. someone's "OpenClaw for marketing" guide → your "OpenClaw for content creation." Repeat what works for you; you can even re-post a winner every few months (people forget).
10K+ — Volume. Now only volume matters: 1–5 posts daily (shitposts, vacation pics, client posts, tips, opinions, takes). You finally have the reach potential to hit millions of views and experiment with virality. Lean into long-form articles (Elon is pushing them). Nutshell: ≥5 posts/day, 1–2 articles/week, 1 thread/week → snowball.
The most difficult part in anything is the beginning. Getting the first 500–1K followers was the hardest thing, even harder from 5K–10K, and so on.

But if you follow the path, iterate a lot, and just do it consistently, the follower count compounds over time.
