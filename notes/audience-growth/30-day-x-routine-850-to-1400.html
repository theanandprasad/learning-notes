The 30-Day X Routine: 850 → 1,400 Followers, 4.9M Views

By Tarang Agarwal (@tarang8811) • Published Apr 13, 2026 • Saved Jun 9, 2026
X / Twitter Growth Replies Distribution Monetization

Thirty days: from 857 followers and 100–200 views a tweet to 4.9M impressions (single-day peak ~1M) and monetized — by doing one thing, not ten.

⚠️ Captured as-is. A personal X-growth playbook; all follower/impression/revenue figures are the author's own claims, unverified, and the platform mechanics he describes are his read of a fast-changing algorithm. Archived as one practitioner's method, not endorsed advice.

The one insight

Originals build your brand. Replies build your reach. Nobody cares about your originals until they know who you are — so stop posting louder into the void and show up where the attention already is: the replies under bigger accounts in your niche. A reply that lands in the top 3 borrows their audience for free.

Where he started

Under 900 followers. A bio that didn't say what he did, a six-month-old pinned tweet, posting 2–3× a week — always originals, always hoping. He'd built a real business ($800K ARR Shopify app, five years of scars) but his account reflected none of it. "Posting into a void and calling it 'consistency.'"

The targets: who to reply to

Where most people blow it: replying to Elon or MrBeast — 50M-follower accounts with 20,000 replies in the first minute, where you'll never be seen. Aim for the sweet spot instead.

Three rules for the list:

  • Their audience is your audience. If their followers wouldn't follow you, nothing you write in their comments matters.
  • They post often enough to give you multiple at-bats per day.
  • You can actually get into the replies while the post is still climbing.

Myth: the 30-minute window

"Reply in the first 30 minutes" no longer holds — posts now run 6–24 hours and can peak anytime. His 500K-view reply wasn't early; it was there when the peak hit. Rule of thumb: catch a tweet within the first 3–6 hours to ride the peak, and just stay consistent in your niche.

The reply that works (and why)

Most replies die not because they're generic but because they never get eyes. What works passes the scroll test: if a stranger reads only your reply, with no context, does it still make them think?

Dies

"Great post!" (adds nothing) · "So true, I wrote about it here [link]" (self-promo, buried) · a 4-paragraph essay (nobody reads it in a comment section)

Lands

A standalone micro-post that happens to live under someone else's tweet. Three lines, four max.

The daily engine (~2 hours, in 3–5 min chunks)

What he stopped doing

  • Scrolling For You "for inspiration." It's a slot machine — 45 minutes for one mediocre idea.
  • Posting threads before anyone cares. Threads are megaphones; earn the audience first.
  • Chasing viral trends outside your topic — it just confuses followers about what you do.
  • Writing originals longer than 3 lines in week one. Short originals test angles in days, not months.
  • Following back everyone — protect your timeline; follow only who you learn from.
  • Replying to out-of-niche viral accounts. A 50K-view reply on a fitness post does nothing for SaaS followers — and may confuse the algorithm about what you are.

What's changed in X monetization

The ground is shifting under the playbook — these are the author's reads of current creator changes:

ShiftWhat it means
Impressions alone no longer payRevenue is moving toward engagement from verified users; optimize for replies & bookmarks, not raw reach.
Engagement rate is a gateBelow ~1% ER the algorithm quietly throttles your next posts. His ER fell 5% → 1.1% over 90 days chasing impressions, and every later post started at a disadvantage.
Monetization requirements500+ followers, verified, and 5M impressions over 3 months. This playbook clears that bar in one month.
Verified replies count more10 likes from verified accounts beat 200 from new ones. Target accounts whose audience skews verified (founders, investors, operators).
X favors original contentThe reply strategy still earns impressions and can cross the payout threshold — but payout from replies is "almost dead." Original content is now king for actually getting paid.

His takeaway: the reply playbook still works to cross the line, but it won't reward like before — after hitting the mark he's pivoting to original content.

The 7-day starter plan

Day 1 — Bio. A stranger should know in 8 seconds who you are, what you do, and what they get. One clear identity (his: "founder who built $800K ARR, building again in public").
Day 2 — Target list. 15+ accounts, all in your niche, all 30K–500K, all posting daily. Save them in a one-click list/folder.
Day 3 — Notifications on for those accounts (delete TikTok — the notifications are your new feed).
Day 4 — Practice. Write 5 reply-shaped micro-posts: three lines each (opinion · specific detail · close). No hashtags, no links.
Day 5 — Ship 10 real replies on live posts; track which land.
Day 6 — First original of the new era: one short story from your life, 3 lines, no moral (let the reader fill it in).
Day 7 — Review analytics. Note the single best reply and best original; next week do more of that shape. Then run it 30 days without changing the plan.

The honest part

4.9 million impressions did not make me rich. It did not ship my product. It did not close a single customer. What it did was buy me a real audience.

The real prize isn't the number — it's the room: an audience of founders and builders who now actually pay attention when he posts about what he's building. Run the playbook because you have something real to say to that room, not for the dopamine of a big number. "But if you want the big number too, 30 days and the playbook above will get you there."
