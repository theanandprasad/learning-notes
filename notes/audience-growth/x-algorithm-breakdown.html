The Key Idea

Every account has a hidden 0–100 reputation score (TweepCred), derived from PageRank over X's social graph. It controls how many people see your tweets. You grow it by earning quality followers and engagement — not raw count — keeping your following under 2,500, and treating replies and relationships as the real engine of growth.

TweepCred: the hidden score

TweepCred is a 0–100 reputation score X assigns every account. It's derived from PageRank — the same idea Google used to rank web pages by how many authoritative pages link to them. On X, "links" are follows: who follows you, and how much weight each of those followers carries. The higher your TweepCred, the more of your audience the algorithm shows each tweet to.

The score is computed by squashing your raw PageRank value into the 0–100 band:

TweepCred = max(0, min(100, round(130 + 5.21 × ln(PageRank))))

He started around 12–15 (tweets pulling 15–50 views) and climbed to roughly 44–48 over the 30 days — crossing from "limited" into "normal algorithmic consideration," which is when growth started compounding.

What moves PageRank (and what doesn't)

Because TweepCred is downstream of PageRank, the question that matters is: which follows actually raise your PageRank? The weight of a follow depends on the follower's own authority and how concentrated their attention is.

Moves it

  • Being followed by high-PageRank accounts
  • Followers who themselves follow very few people (their vote weighs more)
  • Mutual follows with established accounts
  • Engagement from verified / high-authority accounts

Does almost nothing

  • Bot / inactive followers
  • Followers who follow 10,000+ accounts (diluted vote)
  • Raw follower count on its own

The math punchline: 1 real, engaged follower ≈ 10× the PageRank contribution of 10,000 bots. Buying followers is, in his words, "mathematically stupid" — you pay for nodes that pass almost no weight. His approach instead: engage real builders and SaaS founders in the 500–10K follower range, where accounts are authoritative enough to matter but still attentive.

The number that kills accounts: 2,500

Following more than 2,500 accounts triggers a TweepCred penalty defined in X's Reputation.scala. The penalty scales with how far over you are and can reduce your effective reputation by up to ~50×. The cutoff is hard: 2,499 is safe, 2,501 is penalized.

Replies > tweets (RealGraph)

X scores every relationship between two accounts via RealGraph. A reply from someone you have a strong RealGraph score with carries far more weight than one from a stranger — and that weight feeds back into reach. The multipliers below are the author's reading of the model:

RelationshipWeight
Mutual follow + frequent interaction2.5×
Mutual follow, some interaction1.8×
No follow / no interaction1.0× (baseline)

He replied to ~70–80 accounts a day. Two distinct payoffs came from two kinds of replies:

  • Big-account replies (replying under Grok or trending posts) gave raw impressions — one reply hit 155K views, another 101K with 261 likes, for roughly 900K total reply impressions over 30 days.
  • Mid-tier relationships (500–10K builders) drove most of the actual follower growth. Impressions are vanity; relationships are the engine.

What the algorithm boosts & penalizes

The measured multipliers below are per the source, as read and estimated by the author.

Content format

  • Thread (5–10 tweets): +20–30% on the first tweet
  • Image: +10–15%
  • Video: +15–25%
  • External link: −5 to −15% penalty — this is exactly why "link in bio" exists

Verification (Premium)

  • 1.3–1.8× reach
  • 3–4× reply visibility near the top of threads
  • +200–400% search ranking

He subscribed to Premium before starting, treating verification as table stakes.

Account-health killers

  • 4 "Not Interested" clicks in 90 days → ~80% reach reduction
  • Engagement pods → detected and discounted
  • Follow/unfollow churn → 30–50% quality reduction
  • Too-regular posting → flagged as bot-like

The daily playbook

1 post per day, 8–10 PM IST — timed so the US audience is awake.
70–80 replies/day — big accounts for reach, builders for relationships.
10–15 targeted #buildinpublic follows/day — selective, never spray.
Zero external links in tweets — avoid the format penalty.
Zero follow/unfollow — protect account health.

What worked

  • Posts with specific numbers
  • Honest struggle posts
  • Connection posts (replies that start conversations)

What didn't

  • Posting 5×/day (the timeline caps you at 1–2 per load)
  • Generic advice
  • Hashtags
  • Posting when the audience is asleep

The six things the algorithm rewards

  1. Quality over quantity — PageRank-weighted followers, not raw count.
  2. Engagement velocity — what happens in the first 15 minutes.
  3. Relationship depth — replies that themselves get replies.
  4. Staying under 2,500 following — avoid the Reputation.scala penalty.
  5. Threads & images over links — favor formats the algorithm boosts.
  6. Consistency in 1–2 topics — build a legible niche.
