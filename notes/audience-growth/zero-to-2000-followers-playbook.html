The core lesson

You have to give X a reason to discover you — and that only happens if you put in the work first. You reach out, engage, comment; then people start doing the same for you. That feedback loop, run consistently, is the whole playbook. "Growth on X is not luck. It is not the algorithm. It is consistent effort, strong relationships, and showing up every single day."

The honest part: it starts transactional

At the start, this process is transactional. There is no way around it. You are essentially boosting each other's content.

But if you keep going, it changes — and that arc is the real engine:

The snowball effect

At first you push the snowball yourself — artificial momentum. Once it starts rolling, it grows on its own. "That is when the avalanche happens. That is when growth becomes real. That is when 'fake it until you make it' ends."

  • Compounding traction: some posts start hitting 10,000–20,000 views. That doesn't auto-convert to followers, but it puts your name out there — and recognition is what drives follows.
  • Free promotion: people start mentioning and tagging you — "underrated creator" lists, best-in-niche lists, sometimes you become a meme. All of it brings new people to your profile.
  • Followers aren't the main metric: his follower growth slowed, but engagement, reach, and network stayed strong. "Focus on the quality of your connections."

Why most people lose momentum

Here's where most fail: they stop doing what got them there. They stop engaging, stop replying, and blame the algorithm — and growth drops immediately. "What got you to this point must continue. Always."

The daily standard

If you're serious, aim for 100–150 replies per day — that's what maintains momentum, keeps you visible, and strengthens your network. He still pushes close to 100% engagement even now. And never take your audience for granted: "If you help others win, you will win too. That is the long-term game."
