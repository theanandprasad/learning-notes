No "yes." Either "HELL YEAH!" or "no."

By Derek Sivers • Published Aug 26, 2009 • Saved Jun 5, 2026
Decisions Priorities Saying No Focus

If you're often over-committed or too scattered, use this rule: if you're not saying "HELL YEAH!" about something, say no.

The rule

When deciding whether to do something, if you feel anything less than "Wow! That would be amazing! Absolutely! Hell yeah!" — then say no. There is no lukewarm "yes."

Why it works

Saying no to most things leaves room in your life to throw yourself completely into the rare thing that makes you say "HELL YEAH!" Every event you're invited to, every request to start a new project — apply the same filter.

We're all busy. We've all taken on too much. Saying yes to less is the way out.

This short post later became the title of Sivers' book Hell Yeah or No. It pairs naturally with the prestige-and-overcommitment warnings in How to Do What You Love and the priority "shelf system" in How to Pick a Career.
