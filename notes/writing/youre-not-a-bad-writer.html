You're Not a Bad Writer. You're Just Afraid to Be a Writer.

By Aleena Rose · Published Dec 9, 2025 · Saved Jun 5, 2026
Writing Creative Courage Essay Vulnerability

Writing is a quiet art — but the most terrifying part of it means abandoning the cold corner of yourself for the fields, where there are no walls and everyone else is alive.

The heart of it

The block isn't talent — it's fear. To be a writer is to leave the safe interior, the coldest corner of yourself, and walk out into the world to pay attention. It asks the old self to die so there is room for other people, cities, beasts, and characters. The fright you mistake for being a "bad writer" is really the fear of going out into the fields.

01The quiet art and the cold corner

Rose opens with loneliness as the writer's native climate — and then names the dread that follows it. The page is safe; the world is not.

every good writer i've met is the loneliest person in the world.
writing is a quiet art. you're left to sit alone in the coldest corner of yourself, waiting for trains that never come. the whole time, you're honking in the silence.

But the corner is not where the writing finally happens. The terror is in leaving it.

the most terrifying part of being a writer means abandoning the cold corner for the fields. no walls, no ceiling, no shelter (however flimsy it was before).

02Writing as betrayal / abandoning the self

Her friends have a phrase for it. Rose worries the phrase is too gentle — that what's really asked is closer to a death than a misplacement.

my friends call it "abandoning the self," but when something is abandoned, there's the slight opportunity of finding it again. when you are a writer, and the time comes for you to know characters and cities and beasts that you never knew before, the old self must die in order to make space for everyone else.

That dying-to-make-room is physical in her telling — stomach, ears, the whole body offered up to know a stranger or a city. And it is, plainly, a betrayal of the quiet life she'd otherwise keep.

but then the pen says NO RISK, NO STORY and the introvert has to shoulder herself to the street corner and the restaurant table and the sea and the bridge.

The reward is not warmth. She's honest about the cost: "intimacy without warmth and passion without fulfillment."

03It's not so easy writing about nothing

Here the essay turns. The exhaustion is real — but so is the sweetness, the dog pawing at her heels that turns out to be kind. And then a borrowed line snaps her awake.

"it's not so easy writing about nothing." — Patti Smith (quoted in the essay)

Nothing, Rose notes, is the comfortable thing — a brain that sleeps, eyes that close. To write about everything is to carry the circus's swollen weight and never quite close your eyes again. The cure she offers herself isn't to retreat, but to keep stealing from the world and to share what she steals.

forget the cold room — take a hair, an eyelash, a dream from the world, and weave yourself a blanket! share the blanket! you're not a bad writer, you're just afraid to be a writer!

04The thief of the greatest kind

The essay ends not with a method but with a permission slip. The fear gets renamed as what it is, and the taking gets reframed as a gift rather than a crime.

there's no jail time for this kind of theft — embrace it! you're a thief of the greatest kind!
