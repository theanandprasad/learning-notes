How to Become Disgustingly Creative in 2026

By Hussain Ibarra (The Profitable Micro Business) • Published Apr 20, 2026 • Saved Jun 5, 2026
Creativity Focus Flow Writing Business

Creativity is your ability to see what others can't — to connect two unrelated topics so they fit perfectly. It isn't a gift you're born with or without. It's a muscle built through focus and intentional practice.

The thesis in two moves

(1) Focus is the greatest skill of the AI era — it's a trainable, limited resource (your mental "RAM"), and it determines what enters your consciousness. (2) Creativity is curation: chase genuine curiosity, consume with intention, run it through your own lived experience, and iterate. Original ideas are a result of curation, not invention.

Your cognitive load is RAM

Humans process ~185 million bits of information over a lifetime — but most people waste it, living on autopilot and numbing their minds with cheap dopamine. The problem isn't lack of motivation: scrolling, gaming, Netflix are "not nothing — they just amount to nothing." They spend a limited cognitive load on tasks that don't mean anything.

Focus is the greatest skill of the 21st century

In an era where AI can write, code, and design almost anything, focus — knowing what to work on, how, and what to avoid — becomes the decisive skill. With this much leverage, decisions aren't 1:1 anymore; they're 1,000× more crucial. You can build the right product, or spend 3 years building something nobody wants.

Focus is a limited resource — but it can be trained, like any sense:

Attention comes when you are deeply interested in something, for then you love to find out all about it; then your whole mind, your whole being is there.— Jiddu Krishnamurti

What this has to do with creativity

Everything. Creativity is seeing what others can't, connecting unrelated topics so they fit, articulating ideas that make people ask "how the hell did this person say it?" You cultivate it by chasing genuine curiosity and doing it for its own sake — not money, fame, or status. You do it because it's fun; the supporters follow without you trying.

The NASA creativity study

In the 1960s, NASA found 98% of kids aged 4–5 scored as creative geniuses. As adults, only 2% did. Schools and society are built to produce obedient workers, not creatives — because, historically, the most creative people (artists, philosophers, writers) were the most dangerous: they challenged norms and refused to fit in.

Nobody can teach you to be creative — if they could, it would cease to exist. People can share methods and processes, but not how to think; if they did, the thoughts would be theirs, not yours.

Flow: the challenge sweet spot

Creativity comes from flow — effortless effort where hours feel like minutes — and flow only appears when you take on new, interesting challenges just outside your skill level. Too hard → anxiety. Too easy → boredom. The goal is to stretch, not snap.

Dissected, creativity is using both modes of thinking: divergent (explore new knowledge and paths) and convergent (take what works and personalize it to your goals).

The 5-step framework

Hussain's framework for generating ideas and writing content (the engine behind 86M+ views and 29k followers). Everyone starts at a different point and walks a different path.

1 · Pick 2–3 topics or interests. You don't need to be the best at any one. Combine all three and you can become the most qualified person in the world — nobody is as good as you at that exact intersection.
2 · Go up one level & dig deep. Don't be too niche ("molecular plant biology" bores people and runs dry). Go broad — ideas need space. (His "writing online" → the broader umbrella "one-person business," which opens sales, marketing, audience-building, psychology…) Then break into 3–5 subtopics, list people you admire, books, and podcasts per interest. He calls these Brand Domains.
3 · Consume with intention. Most people consume for entertainment — "mental obesity." High-quality input = high-quality output. When you find a great idea, stop consuming and start writing about how it made you feel and why it caught you. (This very letter came from intersecting two books — Flow and Think on These Things — neither about creativity.) Original ideas are a result of curation.
4 · Look for new experiences. Your lived experience is the "original" part of your ideas — and AI can't fake it. Authenticity is rare currency. If your life feels boring, go do something: move abroad broke, quit with no plan, travel, start a business, launch a thing that'll fail, live in the woods. Collect experiences and reflect, so you have something to say.
5 · Refine & iterate. Stop hunting for the perfect idea — you need a bad idea first to have something to iterate on. Most of his newsletters feel incomplete when posted; you can always rewrite later. You don't become a painter watching tutorials — you pick up the brush. Creativity is a muscle; practice makes it stronger.
Original ideas are a result of curation. You consume from multiple sources, across different domains, pick ideas you're interested in, and tie them together with your perspective.

When everyone races toward volume and outsources their writing and thinking to AI, the people who leverage their unique experiences and trained focus will win. Chase curiosity for fun, curate with intention, and keep iterating.
