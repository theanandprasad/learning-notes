A countdown of five videos an ambitious person "needs to watch" — and it gets increasingly more niche as it climbs to #1. Less a productivity playlist than a taste in how to think.

The countdown

#5 Veritasium — The Science of Thinking. ~12 minutes on how your brain actually handles effort. Watch it "before you convince yourself that the answer is always more discipline."
#4 Tim Ferriss & David Senra — How Extreme Winners Think and Win. Senra spent 9 years intensely studying the lives and works of hundreds of history's greatest entrepreneurs; this is ~3 hours of him "saying what they all had in common."
#3 Richard Feynman — The Pleasure of Finding Things Out. A 1981 BBC documentary of a Nobel-winning physicist explaining how he actually thinks. "This isn't productivity advice — it's genuine sauce."
#2 Nick Munoz — Rockefeller's Work Ethic. A breakdown of John D. Rockefeller's biography and how he became the richest American in history.
#1 Michael Sugrue — Marcus Aurelius' Meditations. A collection of lectures by a late philosophy professor on the private journal Marcus Aurelius wrote to himself as he was becoming the most powerful man in Rome.

The through-line isn't "hustle harder" — it's how the best minds actually think: the neuroscience of effort, patterns across great builders, a physicist's curiosity, one titan's discipline, and a Stoic emperor's inner life. The creator's sign-off: "If you liked this video, follow for more."
