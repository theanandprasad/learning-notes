10 Insane Things I Did In My 20s To Become A Millionaire

By Nicolas Cole (@Nicolascole77) • Published Mar 26, 2026 • Saved Jun 9, 2026
Discipline Sacrifice Risk Writing Business

A millionaire by his early 30s "because of the absolute hell I put myself through in my 20s." A list of extreme bets and sacrifices — recounted, not recommended.

⚠️ Captured as-is. A personal hustle/sacrifice memoir that ends as a promotion for the author's ghostwriting course; the income figures are his own claims. Several items (working through hospitalization, near-zero cash, all-in on one asset) are extreme and arguably unhealthy — archived as one person's story and mindset, not as advice to copy.

The throughline

Cole frames his wealth as the compounding payoff of a decade of relentless focus, outsized risk, and personal sacrifice — removing distractions, betting before he was ready, absorbing failures as tuition, and grinding on nights-and-weekends side projects until one of them broke through (writing/ghostwriting).

The ten (in his words, paraphrased)

1 · Cut the internet at home until he finished his first book (Confessions of a Teenage Gamer) — which took ~4 years. Removed the distraction to force the work.
2 · Started his first company at 26 with ~$5,000 in savings and no backup plan.
3 · Recruited a best friend to co-build a ghostwriting agency — and when the friend balked at the risk, removed it for him: Cole worked double-time and paid him a "salary" out of his own savings for ~4 months until they cracked client acquisition.
4 · Worked through being hospitalized — pushed so hard he landed in hospital with shingles, then kept taking sales calls, client calls, and hiring interviews on painkillers. (He frames this as intense; in hindsight it reads as a warning, not a flex.)
5 · Kept almost no cash on hand, repeatedly buying more Bitcoin instead.
6 · Stacked side projects — while working 60+ hrs/week, took on crypto-messaging gigs paid in tokens. "All of them went to 0. Learned a lot though."
7 · Took on investors — sold 20% of the agency to two angels knowing it was likely the wrong call (it was) but that he'd learn a lot (he did).
8 · Took almost no vacation — his first real one (2019) made him realize he was on the wrong path, and that "nobody could get me off the Ferris wheel except me." He came home and shut the agency down, letting 20+ people go on a single Zoom call.
9 · Rebuilt during the pandemic — ghostwrote to pay rent while writing The Art & Business of Online Writing, which he says has since generated $300K+ and sold tens of thousands of copies.
10 · Built Ship 30 for 30 on nights & weekends (with Dickie Bush, who was still at BlackRock) into what he calls a multi-7-figure business.

He notes the first agency hit ~$20,000/month in under 60 days, and says if starting today he'd ghostwrite for leaders on X — which leads into the promo below.

What's actually underneath the list

Read charitably, it's a case study in extreme focus + risk tolerance + learning from failure. Read critically, several items glorify burnout (working through illness, no cash buffer, no rest). The useful takeaway is the mindset, not the martyrdom — and note the 2019 turning point, where the relentless grind itself became the thing he had to walk away from.
