The core idea

Companies have quietly moved from ChatGPT to Claude for serious work. The gap between using Claude and deploying it professionally is worth $50–500/hr. Here are 17 skills that fill that gap — what each one pays, and how to start charging for it.

Why Claude skills pay

Enterprises are increasingly choosing Claude for the work that actually matters: long context windows that swallow whole contracts, stronger reasoning on messy problems, and safer, more controllable outputs. They want it wired into internal tools, doing legal review, running research, and handling customer operations.

But the people inside these companies who can build that — not just chat with the model, but architect, integrate, and ship a reliable system — are rare. That scarcity is the whole business. You are not selling access to Claude (everyone has that). You are selling the hours and judgment to make it work.

How to start this week

A five-day sprint. No course, no waiting.

Day 1 — Pick your 3. Choose three skills that match your background, and write one paragraph on why you're credible at them.
Day 2 — Find the budget. Search LinkedIn for jobs mentioning Claude or Anthropic. Companies hiring for it are already spending — those are your potential clients.
Day 3 — Build proof. Build one demo plus a 2-minute screen recording walking through it.
Day 4 — Reach out. Send 5 outreach messages sharing the demo — not pitching, just showing what you made.
Day 5 — Follow up. Follow up on everything. The money is in the follow-up, not the first message.

The real advantage is time

Everyone has access to the same Claude. The difference isn't the tool — it's the hours in the seat. The window where these skills are still rare won't last. The people who put in the reps now are the ones who get to charge for them later.
