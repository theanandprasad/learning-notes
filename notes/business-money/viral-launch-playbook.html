The thesis

"Launches don't fail because the product is bad. They fail because nobody saw it." Startups burned $100M+ on launch campaigns last year and 86% of those videos didn't crack 50K views. A trending launch is engineered, not lucky: a stop-the-scroll video, the right influencers in the right communities, hand-written copy, the company network, a giveaway, and obsessive real-time tracking — all timed to the algorithm's first hour.

Cited case studies (author's figures): Meridian AI SEO launch 5.2M+ views, Arcads AI ad-maker 4M+, Contra Payments 2.2M+.

Step 2, expanded: two buckets of influencers

ICP accounts (drive sales)

Potential customers or people embedded in your buyer's world. When a marketing-agency CEO posts about your AI ad maker, it carries weight with other marketers. Not random "AI marketing" accounts with 100K followers who post 10×/day.

Amplification accounts (cultural moment)

News pages, media accounts, high-reach creators. When several large credible accounts post the same thing in a short window, it signals X that this is big news — and it gets pushed. "This is how you go trending."

Why it works: the algorithm's first hour

The play is built around how X actually distributes content. The first 30–60 minutes decide everything. X gives every post a small test audience and measures engagement velocity (replies, retweets, quote-tweets, bookmarks relative to time). High ratio → pushed to a massive audience; low ratio → "it's so over." And not all engagement is equal:

SignalWeight
LikesWeakest (+0.5)
Bookmarks / reposts / quote-tweetsStrong velocity signals in the test window
Replies (deep & meaningful, not spam)Highest priority — up to 150× a like

Non-negotiable: the founder replies for 48 hours

"The founder has to reply to everything. Every single comment. Not just the first hour. For 48 hours straight." Every founder reply creates a new reply chain — the #1 ranking signal on X. Clear your calendar; your only job is replying and hitting up connections. Launches where founders go all-in on replies outperform by 2–3×, and posts keep gaining traction for a full week.

Launches don't fail because the product is bad. They fail because nobody saw it.
