The core claim

A launch is engineered, not lucky. Six steps: (1) a video worth stopping for, (2) the right influencers, (3) hand-write every influencer post, (4) use the company network, (5) include a valuable giveaway, (6) track everything. All six are built around how X's algorithm distributes content — the first 30–60 minutes decide everything, replies count far more than likes, and the founder must reply for a full 48 hours.

The six steps

1 · Make a video worth stopping for. The hook is the #1 viral signal. Build swipe files of proven 10M+-view hooks (using tools like 1of10 and Foreplay) and pattern-match them to the product. You have 3 seconds to stop the scroll: land a bold claim by second 5 and a live product demo by second 10, in present tense, with audio and visuals matched 1:1. Write at roughly a 5th-grade reading level. Example: Arcads AI mashed together 5 viral hooks into one video → 4M+ views.
2 · Work with the right influencers. X's algorithm sorts content into "SimClusters" — about 145,000 communities — so the job is to infiltrate the right ones. The formula: Engagement + Audience Overlap / Cultural Impact. Use two buckets: ICP accounts (your buyers' world — these drive sales) and Amplification accounts (news / high-reach — these make it feel like a cultural moment). Several credible accounts posting in a short window signals "big news" to the algorithm. Don't just sort by follower count — you'll get bots and low engagement.
3 · Write every influencer post by hand. Busy creators half-ass AI-generated posts, so supply tailored copy matched to each creator's voice. Require the paid-partner badge. Plant a subtle, slightly provocative edge so a specific community distributes the post for you — by arguing about it. (Noted here neutrally: this is a deliberately manipulative mechanic.)
4 · Use the company network. Tap customers, partners, and employees' personal contacts — then systematize and incentivize it. One company gave staff an extra PTO day for hitting 1,000 comments in 24 hours → 2.9M views.
5 · Include a valuable giveaway. Give away something genuinely useful — your hiring system, sales process, or fundraising strategy. People love free stuff, and it drives the engagement the algorithm rewards.
6 · Track everything. Run a real-time "War Room" — 40+ people monitoring every impression, comment, and repost on launch day. Push more fuel toward whatever's outperforming and course-correct the narrative live.

Why it works: the algorithm

Every step above is reverse-engineered from a few facts about how X actually distributes a post. The window is tiny and the engagement weighting is lopsided.

MechanicHow it works
First 30–60 minutesA small test audience measures engagement velocity. High ratio → a big push to a wider audience. Low ratio → it quietly dies.
Not all engagement is equalLikes are the weakest signal (about +0.5). Replies are the strongest — worth up to roughly 150× — and an author reply is critical.
Founder replies for 48 hrsThe founder must reply to every comment for two full days. Each reply spawns a new reply chain — itself a top ranking signal.
Founder-engaged launchesOutperform by 2–3× and can keep gaining for a full week rather than dying in hours.
Launches don't fail because the product is bad. They fail because nobody saw it.
